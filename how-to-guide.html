How to Run Your Dropshipping Shop

🌍 USA & Global Focus
💳 Stripe Payments
📦 Zero Stock Model
⏱ 30 min to launch

This guide walks you through everything — from setting up your shop file to making your first sale in the USA and shipping worldwide. Your shop is a single HTML file that works as both your storefront and your admin dashboard.

✅How dropshipping works in one sentence: A customer pays you → you forward the order to your supplier → the supplier ships directly to your customer → you keep the difference.

Step 1

Set Up Your Shop

Your shop is the my-shop.html file. It contains your public storefront AND your admin dashboard — everything in one file.

1

Open your shop file

Double-click my-shop.html in your browser. You'll see your storefront. Click ⚙ Admin in the nav to access your dashboard.

2

Name your shop

In Admin → Shop Settings — enter your shop name, hero headline, and subheading. This appears on your storefront instantly.

3

Everything saves automatically

Your products, orders, and payments are saved in your browser's local storage. When you deploy online (Step 4), customers see your storefront but can't access your admin.

⚠️Important: Local storage is browser-specific. If you switch browsers or clear cache, data won't transfer. Once deployed to Netlify, your store is live — but you'll always manage it from your own device where you set it up.
Step 2

Add Products

Go to Admin → Products → + Add Product. Here's what to fill in:

📦

Product Name & Description

Write a clear name (e.g. "Pro Noise-Cancelling Earbuds") and a short description focused on benefits. Good descriptions increase conversions.

💰

Set your price

Enter the supplier cost and your selling price. Aim for at least 60–70% margin to cover ad spend and fees. Use the Profit Calculator in Admin to check before listing.

🔗

Add a Stripe Payment Link

Create a payment link in your Stripe Dashboard first (see Step 3), then paste the URL here. When customers click "Buy Now" they go straight to Stripe checkout.

Where to find winning products

  • AliExpress Dropshipping Center (aliexpress.com/dropshipping) — shows trending products with sales data
  • CJ Dropshipping (cjdropshipping.com) — filter by "Hot Products" and USA warehouse stock
  • TikTok — search #TikTokMadeMeBuyIt for viral products people are already buying
  • Amazon Best Sellers — see what's hot, then find the same/similar on AliExpress cheaper
  • AutoDS (autods.com) — paid tool that scrapes winning products daily
Step 3

Connect Stripe Payments

Stripe is how you get paid. It handles credit cards, debit cards, Apple Pay, Google Pay — everything. It's trusted, fast, and used by millions of businesses.

1

Create your Stripe account

Go to stripe.com → Sign up. You'll need your email, a phone number, and your bank details to receive payouts. Takes about 10 minutes.

2

Verify your identity

Stripe requires ID verification (passport or driving licence) before you can receive payouts. Do this immediately so you're not blocked when sales come in.

3

Create a Payment Link per product

Stripe Dashboard → Payment Links → + New → set the product name and price → Create → copy the buy.stripe.com/… URL → paste it into your product in Admin.

4

Save your API keys

Stripe Dashboard → Developers → API Keys → copy your Publishable key → paste it in Admin → Stripe & Payments → Save.

💡Stripe fees: Stripe charges 2.9% + 30¢ per transaction in the USA. On a $29.99 sale that's about $1.17 — factor this into your pricing. Use the Profit Calculator in Admin.

When you get paid

Stripe pays out to your bank account on a rolling 2-day delay (after your first 7-day waiting period). So if someone buys today, the money lands in your bank in about 2–7 days depending on your account age.

Step 4

Go Live on the Internet (Netlify)

Right now your shop only works on your computer. To make it live so US customers can visit it, you need to host it online. Netlify is free and takes 2 minutes.

1

Go to app.netlify.com

Sign up free with Google or GitHub. No card needed.

2

Drag and drop your file

On the Netlify dashboard, find the "Deploy manually" area. Drag your my-shop.html file onto it. Done.

3

You get a live URL instantly

Netlify gives you a URL like jolly-fox-abc123.netlify.app. Share this with customers. It loads your storefront.

4

Get a custom domain (optional but recommended)

Buy a .com domain from Namecheap (~$10/year) or GoDaddy. In Netlify → Domain Settings → add custom domain → follow DNS instructions. Looks far more professional to US customers.

⚠️Important: When deployed online, your store's products (added via Admin) are saved in your local browser — not on the server. Your storefront shows what's in your local browser's storage. To keep products in sync, always manage the shop from the same device and browser you set it up on. For a more advanced setup later, consider a backend like Supabase (you've already used this for ClientTech).

Selling in the USA 🇺🇸

The US is the world's biggest e-commerce market. Here's exactly what you need to know:

Pricing in USD

Your shop is already set to show $ prices. When setting prices, research what similar products sell for on Amazon US and price 10–30% below to be competitive.

Shipping expectations

⚠️American customers expect delivery in 5–10 days. Anything longer causes complaints and refund requests. Always use CJ Dropshipping's US warehouse or Zendrop for US orders. Never use standard AliExpress shipping (15–30 days) for US customers.

Top-selling niches in the USA right now

  • 🏠 Home & kitchen gadgets — US homes are large, people love home products
  • 💪 Fitness & wellness — massive market, especially post-COVID
  • 🐕 Pet accessories — Americans spend $150B/year on pets
  • 👶 Baby & kids products — parents buy constantly, repeat customers
  • 💄 Beauty & skincare — huge TikTok-driven demand
  • 📱 Phone accessories — high volume, cheap to ship, easy impulse buy

How to accept USD via Stripe

Stripe automatically handles currency conversion. When you set up a Payment Link in Stripe, set the price in USD ($). Stripe deposits the equivalent in your local currency (after conversion) into your bank account.

Returns & customer service

  • Offer a clear 30-day return policy — US customers expect this
  • Set up a contact email (use Gmail to start) and respond within 24 hours
  • For disputes/refunds: most suppliers will resend or refund you if the product is faulty
  • Use Stripe's built-in dispute handling for chargebacks

Handling Orders Day-to-Day

Here's your exact workflow every time a customer orders:

1

Customer pays via Stripe

You get an email from Stripe confirming payment. The money is in your Stripe account immediately (hits your bank in 2 days).

2

Log the order in Admin

Go to Admin → Orders → + Log Order. Enter the customer name, product, your sale price, and what you paid the supplier. This tracks your profit.

3

Place the order with your supplier

Go to your supplier's website (CJ, Zendrop, etc.), place the order with the customer's shipping address. Pay the supplier cost from your Stripe funds.

4

Send the customer their tracking number

Your supplier gives you a tracking number. Email it to your customer. This prevents "where's my order?" messages and builds trust.

5

Update the order status in Admin

Change the order status from Processing → Shipped → Delivered. Also log the payment in Admin → Stripe & Payments with the Stripe payment reference from your Stripe dashboard.

Setting the Right Prices

Use this formula for every product before you list it:

Your Price = (Supplier Cost × 3) minimum
Example: Supplier cost $8 → Sell for at least $24.99
This covers Stripe fees (2.9%), ad spend, and still leaves ~60% margin.

Getting Customers

Having a shop isn't enough — you need to drive traffic to it. Here's what actually works for US dropshipping:

Free methods (start here)

  • TikTok organic — Film short videos showing your product in use. No face needed. Trending sounds + product demo = viral potential. US TikTok has huge buying intent.
  • Instagram Reels — Same content as TikTok. Post your product videos as Reels. Add your shop link in bio.
  • Facebook Groups — Join US buy/sell groups relevant to your niche. Post your products genuinely.
  • Pinterest — Great for home, fashion, beauty. Americans browse Pinterest for shopping inspiration. Free traffic.
  • Reddit — Post in relevant subreddits (r/deals, r/frugal, niche subs). Be helpful, not spammy.

Paid methods (once you're profitable)

  • TikTok Ads — Spark Ads (boosting organic videos) work best. Start with $20/day budget targeting USA. Best ROI in e-commerce right now.
  • Facebook/Instagram Ads — Detailed US targeting. Start with $10/day. Use video ads. Test 3–5 products and double down on what works.
  • Google Shopping Ads — Intent-based. People searching "buy wireless earbuds" are ready to purchase. Higher conversion rate.
💡Best free strategy: Post 3 TikTok videos per day showing your products. One of them will go viral eventually. When it does, make sure your Stripe payment link is in your bio. This is how many dropshippers make their first $1,000.

Legal & Tax (USA Sales)

⚠️This is general guidance, not legal advice. Consult an accountant for your specific situation.

Do you need a US company?

No — you can sell to US customers from outside the USA using Stripe. However, once you're making consistent revenue, consider forming a US LLC (especially a Wyoming or Delaware LLC) which can reduce taxes and increase customer trust. Services like Stripe Atlas or Northwest Registered Agent can set one up for ~$100.

US Sales Tax

Sales tax in the USA is complex — it varies by state. As a non-US seller with no physical presence in the US, you generally don't need to collect sales tax until you hit economic nexus thresholds (usually $100,000 in sales or 200 transactions per state). When you reach that level, use TaxJar or Avalara to automate it. Stripe can also collect and remit tax automatically via Stripe Tax.

Income tax

Profit from your shop is taxable income in your home country. Keep records of all revenue, supplier costs, and fees. Talk to an accountant once you're earning consistently.

Business registration

In the UK, if this is your main business, register as a Sole Trader with HMRC (free, takes 10 minutes at gov.uk). Once over the VAT threshold (£90,000/year), register for VAT.

FAQ

Do I need to hold any stock?

No. That's the whole point of dropshipping. You only buy from the supplier after the customer pays you.

What if a customer wants a refund?

Issue a refund via Stripe (takes 2 clicks). For the supplier cost: most suppliers will refund or resend if the item is faulty or didn't arrive. For "changed my mind" returns, you don't have to refund the original shipping but it's good practice.

How long does shipping take?

Using CJ Dropshipping's US warehouse: 5–8 days. Zendrop: 3–7 days. Standard AliExpress to USA: 15–30 days (avoid this for US customers).

Can I run this alongside another job?

Yes. Most dropshippers start part-time. Order fulfilment takes 15–30 minutes a day once you have a system. Scale to full-time once revenue justifies it.

What if someone copies my shop?

They can copy the look, but your supplier relationships, customer base, and brand reputation are yours. Focus on building a brand rather than just selling generic products.

How do I handle US customer service from the UK?

Email is fine — US/UK time difference means you can respond to morning US emails in your evening. Aim to respond within 24 hours. Use a Gmail auto-responder to confirm receipt of messages immediately.

Should I use my real name/address on the shop?

You need a contact email but not a physical address to start. Once you form a company, use the registered address. Never use your home address publicly.
